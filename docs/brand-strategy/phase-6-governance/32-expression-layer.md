# 32. Expression Layer

## ALLONE Brand Governance — Expression Architecture

This document defines how the ALLONE brand expresses itself visually, verbally, and behaviorally. Expression is where strategy becomes tangible. The emotional layer defines what people should feel. The strategic layer defines what the brand stands for. The expression layer defines how all of that materializes in specific decisions, word choices, and behavioral patterns. Without codified expression, brand consistency depends on individual judgment, and individual judgment drifts. This document eliminates drift.

---

## 1. Visual Expression

A visitor forms an opinion within three seconds of encountering any ALLONE touchpoint. These guidelines ensure that opinion is consistent regardless of who designed the asset.

### Logo Usage

- **Website header**: Left-aligned in the floating navigation pill, rendered in Dark Navy `#071D2F`. No tagline. Links to homepage. Never smaller than 24px height.
- **Social profiles**: Logomark (icon only) centered on white `#FFFFFF` background with 20% canvas padding on all sides for square crops.
- **Presentations**: Title slide (centered), subsequent slides (bottom-left), closing slide (centered). Dark Navy on light backgrounds, white on dark. Never on busy imagery without a solid backing shape.
- **Email signatures**: 120px max width, followed by name, title, one line of contact. No social icons. The logo is the only visual.
- **Documents/proposals**: Top-left on page one, bottom-center on subsequent footers. Always Dark Navy. Never below 80px width.

**Protection zone**: Clear space equal to the height of the "A" on all sides. **Never**: reduce contrast, rotate, distort, add effects, or recolor outside the approved palette.

### Color Application

**Primary — Electric Blue `#0A68F5`**: The accent color. CTA buttons, hyperlinks, active navigation states, key statistics, progress indicators. Never as a background fill for large areas. It accents and punctuates.

**Secondary — Surface Tints `#F8FAFE` / `#F1F6FB` / `#E0EEFB`**: Create depth without new colors. Section backgrounds alternate white and `#F8FAFE`. Cards use `#F1F6FB` default, `#E0EEFB` for hover/active. Lightest for large areas, deepest for focused elements.

**Dark — Navy `#071D2F`**: The anchor. Body text, headings, footer background, modal overlays. The counterweight to white that prevents the palette from feeling weightless.

### Typography in Practice

**General Sans — Headings**: H1 through H4, hero text, feature titles. Tight letter-spacing (`-0.02em` to `-0.04em`). Semibold (600) for H1/H2, Medium (500) for H3/H4. Line height: 1.1 to 1.2.

**Plus Jakarta Sans — Body/UI**: Paragraphs, navigation, buttons, forms. Relaxed spacing (default or `+0.01em`). Line height: 1.6 to 1.7 for body, 1.4 for UI. Regular (400) for text, Medium (500) for labels.

**JetBrains Mono — Code/Accents**: Code snippets, status labels, section eyebrows (e.g., "OUR SERVICES"). Uppercase, Regular (400), wide letter-spacing (`+0.08em` to `+0.12em`), 11px to 13px size.

### Photography, Illustration, Icons, Layout

- **Photography**: People-focused, candid, bright with natural light. Tech appears naturally but people are the hero. No stock aesthetics. Color harmony with the blue-white-navy system.
- **Illustration**: Minimal line art, single-weight strokes, geometric forms. Dark Navy or Electric Blue lines, never both together. Convergence motifs (elements merging, inputs unifying). No cartoon elements.
- **Icons**: Lucide library exclusively. 1.5-2px stroke, outline only. Dark Navy default, Electric Blue for active states. Sizes: 20px inline, 24px cards, 32-40px feature blocks.
- **Layout**: 80-120px vertical section spacing. One visual anchor per section. 12-column grid, 24px gutters. Max width: 1200px text, 1440px full sections. Single column on mobile with 16px padding.

## 2. Verbal Expression

### Writing Style

- **Active voice always**: "We built an automation pipeline" not "An automation pipeline was built."
- **Short sentences**: 12 to 18 words average. If a sentence has a comma and conjunction, split it.
- **Specific numbers**: "Reduced processing time by 73%" not "Significantly reduced processing time."
- **Conversational but professional**: Write as if explaining to a smart friend who owns a business. No jargon. No corporate stiffness.
- **No filler**: Eliminate "very," "really," "actually," "basically," "just," and "simply."
- **Present tense**: "ALLONE builds custom AI solutions" not "ALLONE will build."

### Headline Formula

**Benefit + How**: "Cut operational costs by 90% with AI-native automation" / "One partner for every automation need"

**Bold Claim + Proof**: "Enterprise-grade AI at startup prices. 5 clients already agree." / "Georgia's first AI convergence agency. Now in Belgium."

No headline exceeds 12 words. Subheadlines carry the rest.

### CTA Language

- **Primary**: "Start automating", "Book a free call", "See it in action", "Get your AI roadmap"
- **Secondary**: "Learn how it works", "View case studies", "Explore services"
- **Avoid**: "Submit", "Click here", "Learn more", "Contact us", "Get started"

Every CTA answers: "What do I get by clicking this?"

### Error and Empty State Language

- **Error**: "Something went wrong on our end. Try again in a moment." Not: "Error 500."
- **Empty state**: "No projects here yet. Your first project will appear right here." Not: "No data found."
- **404**: "This page doesn't exist, but we do. Head back home or reach out."

Errors are ALLONE's responsibility. Never the user's fault.

### Email Tone

Professional warm. Skip "Hope this finds you well." Start with the point. Short paragraphs, bullet points for lists, bold for key dates. Close with one clear next step: "Let me know if Thursday at 2pm works." Signature: name, title, phone, logo. Nothing else.

### Social Media Voice

- **LinkedIn**: Founder perspective, data-backed insights, 800-1,200 characters. First line hooks. Max three hashtags.
- **Instagram**: Visual-first. Short captions. Stories daily, feed posts 2-3x/week.
- **X**: Sharp, direct, slightly opinionated. Threads for depth. Quote-tweet with value.
- **Universal rule**: If you remove the brand name and it could be anyone's post, rewrite it.

## 3. Behavioral Expression

Behavior is where expectations are fulfilled or broken.

### Response Time

- **Business hours (10:00-19:00 Tbilisi)**: Substantive response within 2 hours. Not an auto-reply.
- **Off hours**: Within 24 hours. Urgent issues (system down): within 1 hour regardless.
- **Social media**: Comments/DMs within 4 hours during business hours.
- **Rule**: If you cannot answer fully, acknowledge with a specific follow-up time. Silence is always worse.

### Meeting Culture

- **Prepared**: Agenda shared 2+ hours in advance. No purpose, no meeting.
- **Concise**: Default 25 minutes, not 30. If it can be an email, it should be.
- **Action-oriented**: Documented action items, owners, deadlines. No action items means it should have been a document.
- **Punctual**: Start on time. Latecomers miss what they miss.

### Client Communication

- **Proactive updates**: Weekly on active projects, even when the update is "On track."
- **No surprises**: Risks communicated the moment they materialize, not the day before deadline.
- **Single point of contact**: One primary contact per client, responsible for all communication.
- **Documentation**: Every decision and scope change confirmed in writing within 24 hours.

### Problem Resolution

- **Own it**: No blame-shifting. "We missed the deadline" not "The API had an outage."
- **Fix it fast**: Resolution first, explanation second.
- **Learn from it**: Internal retrospective on significant issues. Document prevention measures.
- **Compensate when appropriate**: Discount, extra feature, or expedited delivery when ALLONE's error caused impact. Apologies are words. Compensation is action.

### Sales Behavior

- **Consultative**: First conversation is about their business, not ALLONE's capabilities.
- **Educate not sell**: If AI is not the right solution, say so.
- **No manufactured urgency**: No countdown timers or "limited spots" pressure.
- **Follow up with value**: Every touchpoint includes something useful, never just "checking in."
- **Respect the no**: Thank them, leave the door open. No guilt, no persistent re-engagement.

## 4. Expression Consistency Checklist

Audit quarterly. Every "No" triggers immediate remediation.

- [ ] Logo appears correctly (version, clear space, approved colors) across all touchpoints
- [ ] Electric Blue used exclusively for interactive elements and CTAs, never as large-area background
- [ ] Typography roles maintained: General Sans for headings, Plus Jakarta Sans for body, JetBrains Mono for code/accents only
- [ ] All icons from Lucide library with uniform stroke weight
- [ ] Photography is candid, bright, people-focused, stock-free
- [ ] Voice reads as the same entity across website, email, social, and proposals
- [ ] All CTAs use action-oriented, benefit-first language
- [ ] 95%+ of client inquiries received substantive response within the SLA window
- [ ] All client meetings had agendas, started on time, produced action items
- [ ] Error messages and empty states use helpful, blame-free language
- [ ] Social posts are identifiably ALLONE, not interchangeable with any agency
- [ ] Proposals follow approved template with correct branding
- [ ] Website maintains generous whitespace standards
- [ ] Footer anchors every page in Dark Navy `#071D2F`

## 6. Common Mistakes

**Electric Blue as background.** Blue accents. The moment it fills a section, it loses power and ALLONE looks like every other tech company.

**Mixing typeface roles.** Plus Jakarta Sans in a heading, General Sans in a paragraph. Each typeface has one job. When roles blur, visual hierarchy collapses.

**Headlines that are descriptions.** "Our AI-Powered Automation Services for Businesses" is a description. "Cut your costs by 90%." is a headline. Headlines claim. Descriptions explain.

**Generic CTAs.** "Learn More" answers nothing. Every CTA must answer "What do I get by clicking this?"

**Response delays without acknowledgment.** Six hours of silence is worse than a one-minute acknowledgment followed by a six-hour wait. Clients need evidence they were heard, not instant answers.

**Overly casual social media.** Conversational is fine. Meme formats, viral trends, and engagement bait ("Agree or disagree?") are not. Substance first.

**Stock photography.** One stock photo invalidates every real photo beside it. Use illustration or empty space instead.

**Inconsistent email signatures.** One format. One template. No motivational quotes, no social icon arrays, no exceptions.

**Pressure in follow-ups.** "Just checking in" is noise. Every follow-up delivers value or asks a specific question. If neither exists, wait.

**Breaking whitespace for density.** Whitespace is confidence. Cramped layouts signal desperation, not thoroughness. Resist the instinct to fill.

## Summary

Expression is where the ALLONE brand becomes real. Every visual decision, sentence, response time, and meeting format either reinforces or undermines the brand. The test: remove the ALLONE logo from any touchpoint. Would someone familiar with the brand still recognize it? If yes, the expression layer is working. If no, something here has been violated, and it needs correction immediately.
