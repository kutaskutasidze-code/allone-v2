# 29. PESO Media Strategy

## Earned, Paid, Shared & Owned Media Framework

---

## 1. PESO Model Overview

The PESO model — Paid, Earned, Shared, and Owned media — provides a unified framework for how a brand communicates, distributes content, and generates demand across every available channel. For a B2B AI automation agency like ALLONE, an integrated PESO approach is not optional but foundational. The reason is structural: B2B purchasing decisions involve multiple stakeholders, longer evaluation cycles, and a higher threshold of trust than consumer transactions. No single channel closes a deal. The client who signs a $15,000 AI workflow contract has likely encountered the brand five to twelve times across different media before engaging sales.

ALLONE operates from Tbilisi with a Belgium office, targeting both the Georgian market and the broader EU. These are fundamentally different media environments. Georgian business media is concentrated, relationship-driven, and accessible. EU tech media is saturated, competitive, and requires earned credibility before granting coverage. A PESO strategy allows ALLONE to leverage its Georgian media advantage for earned coverage while building owned and shared assets that travel across borders without translation friction.

The core thesis: Owned media builds the foundation. Shared media amplifies it. Earned media validates it. Paid media accelerates it. None work in isolation. A blog post (owned) shared by the founder on LinkedIn (shared) that gets picked up by Forbes Georgia (earned) and then retargeted to website visitors (paid) is a single piece of content working across all four channels. This compounding effect is where ALLONE's limited marketing budget outperforms competitors spending five times more on disconnected tactics.

**Why PESO matters specifically for an AI agency:**

- **Trust deficit**: AI services are intangible and clients fear overpromising. Earned media (press, awards, third-party validation) reduces perceived risk more effectively than any ad.
- **Education requirement**: Most prospects do not yet understand what AI automation can do for their business. Owned media (blog, case studies, webinars) fills this knowledge gap before sales conversations begin.
- **Network effects**: B2B decisions are influenced by peer behavior. Shared media — especially LinkedIn — creates visible social proof when prospects see their peers engaging with ALLONE content.
- **Long sales cycles**: A 30-90 day B2B sales cycle means paid media alone burns budget without conversion. Retargeting and nurture sequences (paid + owned) keep ALLONE present throughout the evaluation period.

## 2. Paid Media

### Strategy

Paid media is the accelerant, not the engine. ALLONE uses paid channels to amplify content that has already proven organic traction, retarget engaged visitors, and capture high-intent search demand. The goal is never cold traffic for its own sake but qualified pipeline generation with measurable cost per acquisition.

### LinkedIn Ads (Primary — 60% of Paid Budget)

LinkedIn is the single most important paid channel for B2B AI services. The targeting granularity — by job title, company size, industry, seniority — means ALLONE can reach decision-makers directly rather than filtering through irrelevant traffic.

**Campaign types:**

| Campaign | Objective | Format | Target Audience |
|----------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Brand Awareness | Impressions, video views | Sponsored video, carousel | CEOs, CTOs, ops directors at 50-500 employee companies |
| Lead Generation | Form submissions | Lead gen forms with gated content | Same as above, narrowed by industry |
| Retargeting | Website conversions | Sponsored content, message ads | Website visitors (past 90 days), video viewers (past 180 days) |
| Case Study Promotion | Engagement, clicks | Single image + carousel | Lookalike audiences based on closed clients |
| Event Promotion | Registrations | Event ads | Georgian market for local events, EU for webinars |

**LinkedIn Ad Specifications:**

- Sponsored Content: 1200x627 images, under 150 characters intro text for mobile
- Lead Gen Forms: Maximum 3 fields (name, email, company). Every additional field reduces conversion by 15-20%
- Retargeting: Minimum audience size of 300 matched members. Segment by page visited (pricing page visitors get different creative than blog readers)
- Budget: Start at $50/day, scale winning campaigns to $150/day
- Bidding: Manual CPC for lead gen ($8-$15 target CPC in Georgia, $15-$35 in EU), automated for awareness

### Google Ads (30% of Paid Budget)

Google captures demand that already exists — people actively searching for AI automation services. This is the highest-intent traffic available but also the most competitive on EU keywords.

**Keyword strategy:**

| Keyword Category | Examples | Est. CPC (Georgia) | Est. CPC (EU) | Intent Level |
|-----------------|----------|--------------------:|---------------:|--------------|
| Service-specific | "AI automation services", "chatbot development company" | $2-$5 | $8-$20 | High |
| Location-modified | "AI agency Georgia", "chatbot development Tbilisi" | $1-$3 | N/A | High |
| Problem-aware | "automate customer support", "reduce manual data entry" | $3-$8 | $10-$25 | Medium-High |
| Competitor | "alternative to [competitor]", "[competitor] pricing" | $5-$12 | $15-$30 | High |
| Educational | "what is AI automation", "AI for small business" | $0.50-$2 | $3-$8 | Low |

**Campaign structure:**

- Search campaigns: High-intent service keywords, location-targeted (Georgia + Belgium + target EU countries)
- Performance Max: Retargeting across Google's network (Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover)
- YouTube: Pre-roll ads on AI/business content for brand awareness (low priority, phase 2)

### Retargeting (Cross-Platform)

Every website visitor who does not convert on the first visit enters a retargeting sequence. The average B2B buyer needs 7-13 touchpoints before engaging. Retargeting provides those touchpoints at low cost.

**Retargeting segments:**

| Segment | Definition | Channel | Creative | Duration |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|----------|
| Hot | Visited pricing or contact page | LinkedIn + Google | Direct CTA, consultation offer | 30 days |
| Warm | Read 2+ blog posts or viewed case study | LinkedIn + Google | Case study, social proof | 60 days |
| Cool | Visited homepage only, bounced | LinkedIn | Brand awareness, educational content | 90 days |
| Newsletter | Email subscribers not yet clients | LinkedIn (matched) | Exclusive content, event invites | Ongoing |

### Budget Allocation & Expected Performance

**Monthly budget recommendation by stage:**

| Revenue Stage | Monthly Paid Budget | LinkedIn | Google | Other |
|--------------|--------------------:|---------:|-------:|------:|
| Pre-revenue / Early ($0-$5K/mo) | $2,000 | $1,200 | $600 | $200 |
| Growth ($5K-$20K/mo) | $3,500 | $2,100 | $1,050 | $350 |
| Scale ($20K-$50K/mo) | $5,000 | $3,000 | $1,500 | $500 |
| Expansion ($50K+/mo) | $8,000-$12,000 | 60% | 30% | 10% |

**Expected customer acquisition cost (CAC):**

| Market | Qualified Lead Cost | SQL-to-Close Rate | Effective CAC |
|--------|--------------------:|-------------------:|--------------:|
| Georgia | $200-$500 | 25-35% | $800-$1,500 |
| Belgium / EU | $500-$1,500 | 15-25% | $2,000-$6,000 |

These CAC figures are viable given ALLONE's average contract value of $5,000-$25,000 with 60-80% gross margins. A $3,000 CAC on a $15,000 contract with 70% margin yields $7,500 gross profit — a 2.5x return on acquisition cost.

## 3. Earned Media

### PR Strategy

Earned media is the most credible and the most difficult to control. For ALLONE, the strategy is to make the company easy to write about by providing journalists with compelling narratives, data, and access. The founder's story — 22 years old, raising $1.24M, AI agency in Georgia expanding to Belgium — is inherently newsworthy. The task is packaging and distributing it.

### Story Angles (Ranked by Newsworthiness)

1. **"22-year-old Georgian founder raises $1.24M to build AI agency disrupting traditional consulting"** — Human interest + funding milestone. Works for startup press, business media, and local Georgian outlets.
2. **"Georgian AI startup opens Belgium office with zero upfront cost through revenue-share model"** — Business strategy angle. Interesting to startup media covering unconventional expansion.
3. **"How AI automation drops service delivery costs 90% — and why emerging market agencies will eat incumbents"** — Thought leadership / industry disruption angle. Works for tech publications, AI-focused media.
4. **"From TBC Bank analyst to AI founder: How Georgia's tech ecosystem is producing a new generation of builders"** — Ecosystem angle. Appeals to Georgian media, emerging market tech coverage.
5. **"AI agency uses commission-based sales army of 30 instead of traditional marketing"** — Go-to-market innovation angle. Relevant to sales/marketing publications.

### Target Publications

| Tier | Publication | Angle | Approach | Timeline |
|------|------------|-------|----------|----------|
| **Tier 1 (Local)** | Forbes Georgia | Founder profile, funding | Direct pitch to editor, leverage existing connections | Month 1-2 |
| **Tier 1 (Local)** | Business Media Georgia | Company profile, market disruption | Press release + interview offer | Month 1-2 |
| **Tier 1 (Local)** | Marketer.ge | AI industry analysis | Guest article + company mention | Month 2-3 |
| **Tier 2 (Regional)** | EU-Startups.com | Funding announcement, expansion | Standard pitch via submissions form | Month 2-4 |
| **Tier 2 (Regional)** | ArcticStartup | Eastern European tech growth | Pitch via journalist outreach | Month 3-5 |
| **Tier 2 (Regional)** | Tech.eu | Belgium office + AI disruption | Contributed article or interview | Month 3-6 |
| **Tier 3 (Aspirational)** | TechCrunch | Only with major milestone ($5M+ raise, marquee client) | Warm intro via investors/network | Year 2+ |
| **Tier 3 (Aspirational)** | The Verge / Wired | AI industry trend piece with ALLONE as case study | Respond to journalist queries, HARO | Year 2+ |

### Speaking Engagements

| Event Type | Examples | Goal | Frequency |
|-----------|---------|------|-----------|
| Startup events | StartupGeorgia Demo Days, Startup Grind Tbilisi | Brand visibility, local network | Monthly |
| Tech conferences | Tbilisi Tech Week, Web Summit (Lisbon), How to Web (Bucharest) | EU credibility, lead gen | Quarterly |

### Awards Strategy

Apply systematically to build third-party validation:

- **Georgia**: StartupGeorgia awards, GITA innovation awards, Forbes Georgia 30 Under 30
- **Regional**: EU-Startups Top 10, Central & Eastern European Startup Awards
- **Industry**: AI Breakthrough Awards, Stevie Awards for Technology, Clutch Top AI Companies
- **Timeline**: Submit to 2-3 awards per quarter. Budget $500/year for application fees.

### Media Kit Contents

Maintain a downloadable press kit at allone.ge/press containing:

- Company fact sheet (one page: founding date, team size, funding, offices, key metrics)
- Founder bio and professional headshots (3 versions: formal, casual, speaking)
- Company logos (SVG, PNG, dark/light variants)
- Product screenshots and demo videos
- Key statistics and client results (anonymized if needed)
- Approved quotes from the founder
- Contact information for press inquiries

### Channel Strategy

| Channel | Audience | Effort Allocation | Content Type | Posting Frequency |

| **LinkedIn** (Company) | B2B prospects, partners, investors | 40% | Case studies, insights, company updates | 4-5x/week |

| **Facebook** | Georgian market specifically | 5% | Repurposed LinkedIn content, event promotion | 2-3x/week |
| **Twitter/X** | Tech community, AI discourse | 5% | Hot takes, thread breakdowns, engagement with AI leaders | Daily |
| **YouTube** | Long-form seekers, tutorial audience | 5% | Tutorials, case study videos, webinar recordings | 2-4x/month (planned) |

### LinkedIn Deep Dive (75-85% of Social Effort)

LinkedIn is where ALLONE's revenue lives. The strategy splits between the company page (institutional credibility) and the founder's personal profile (human connection, thought leadership). The founder's personal content will consistently outperform the company page in reach and engagement — this is by design, not a problem to solve.

**Company page content pillars:**
- Client results and case studies (30%)
- AI industry insights and trends (25%)
- Product/service announcements (20%)
- Company culture and hiring (15%)
- Event and partnership announcements (10%)

**Founder personal content pillars:**
- Lessons from building ALLONE — raw, specific, non-generic (35%)
- AI industry opinions — contrarian takes backed by data (25%)
- Client transformation stories — with permission, detailed before/after (20%)
- Georgian tech ecosystem commentary (10%)
- Personal journey and behind-the-scenes (10%)

**Engagement protocol:**
- Respond to every comment on own posts within 2 hours
- Comment thoughtfully on 10-15 posts from target prospects and industry leaders daily
- Send personalized connection requests to engaged commenters (not a sales pitch — genuine engagement first)
- Share and comment on client and partner content weekly

### Employee Advocacy Program

Every team member becomes a distribution channel. This multiplies ALLONE's LinkedIn reach without additional ad spend.

**Program structure:**
- Provide team with a shared content library (pre-approved posts, images, talking points) updated weekly
- Encourage but do not mandate personal sharing — authenticity matters more than compliance
- Celebrate top-performing employee posts in team meetings
- Budget: $0 direct cost. Time investment: 1 hour/week to prepare shared content library
- Expected impact: 3-5x increase in organic LinkedIn impressions within 3 months

### Community Participation

Actively contribute to relevant communities rather than broadcasting at them:

- **AI communities**: r/artificial, AI-focused Discord servers, Hugging Face community
- **Startup groups**: Startup Grind, Y Combinator forums (lurk and contribute, do not pitch)
- **Georgian tech**: Local Telegram groups, Facebook groups for Georgian startups
- **LinkedIn groups**: AI in Business, Digital Transformation Leaders, B2B Marketing
- **Rule**: 80% value-adding comments and answers, 20% subtle brand mention. Never lead with a pitch.

## 7. Budget Split Across PESO

### Year 1 Budget Allocation

Year 1 prioritizes building owned assets and growing shared presence because these are compounding investments. Paid is used surgically, not as the primary growth engine. Earned media efforts are relationship-building that pays off in Year 2.

| Category | % of Marketing Budget | Allocation (on $5K/mo total) | Focus |
|----------|----------------------:|-----------------------------:|-------|
| **Owned** | 40% | $2,000/mo | Website optimization, blog production, email setup, case study creation, ROI calculator build |
| **Shared** | 30% | $1,500/mo | Content creation for LinkedIn, community management, employee advocacy setup, video production |
| **Paid** | 20% | $1,000/mo | LinkedIn Ads (lead gen + retargeting), initial Google Ads testing |
| **Earned** | 10% | $500/mo | PR outreach, press kit creation, award applications, speaking event logistics |

### Year 2 Budget Allocation

By Year 2, owned assets are built and producing returns. Paid scales up with proven campaigns. Earned media relationships from Year 1 begin generating coverage.

| Category | % of Marketing Budget | Allocation (on $10K/mo total) | Focus |
|----------|----------------------:|------------------------------:|-------|
| **Owned** | 30% | $3,000/mo | Content scaling, webinar program, course development, platform features |
| **Shared** | 25% | $2,500/mo | Video production, YouTube channel, expanded employee advocacy, community leadership |
| **Paid** | 25% | $2,500/mo | Scaled LinkedIn campaigns, Google Ads expansion, retargeting across platforms |
| **Earned** | 20% | $2,000/mo | PR agency or freelancer, conference sponsorships, award submissions, podcast tour |

### Budget Scaling Triggers

Do not scale budget linearly. Scale based on performance signals:

- **Increase paid budget when**: Cost per qualified lead is below $400 (Georgia) or $1,000 (EU) and pipeline is converting
- **Increase owned budget when**: Blog posts are generating organic traffic above 500 visits/month and email list exceeds 1,000 subscribers
- **Increase earned budget when**: First 3-5 press placements are secured and generating measurable referral traffic
- **Increase shared budget when**: LinkedIn engagement rate exceeds 3% consistently and founder's follower count surpasses 5,000

## 8. Attribution Model

### The B2B Attribution Problem

B2B sales cycles for AI services run 30-90 days. A client who signs a $20,000 contract may have first seen a LinkedIn post 4 months ago, read 3 blog posts, clicked a retargeting ad, attended a webinar, and then booked a consultation through a direct email. Attributing that sale to a single channel is misleading. Multi-touch attribution is required.

### Attribution Framework

ALLONE uses a three-layer attribution model:

**Layer 1 — First Touch (How They Found Us)**

Captures the channel that introduced the prospect to ALLONE for the first time. Critical for understanding which channels drive top-of-funnel awareness.

| First Touch Source | Tracking Method |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Organic search | UTM parameters, Google Search Console |
| LinkedIn (organic) | UTM on all shared links, LinkedIn analytics |
| LinkedIn Ads | Campaign tracking, UTM |
| Google Ads | Google Ads conversion tracking, UTM |
| Referral | Referral URL tracking |
| Press/PR | Custom UTM per publication |
| Direct | Assume brand awareness from offline or untracked |
| Event | Custom landing pages per event |

**Layer 2 — Last Touch (What Converted Them)**

Captures the final interaction before the prospect booked a consultation or submitted a lead form. Critical for understanding which channels close.

**Layer 3 — Weighted Multi-Touch Attribution**

For deals above $5,000 or sales cycles longer than 30 days, apply weighted attribution across all touchpoints:

| Touchpoint Position | Weight |
|--------------------|-------:|
| First touch | 30% |
| Lead creation (form fill, email subscribe) | 20% |
| Middle touches (blog visits, ad clicks, email opens) | 10% each (max 3 counted) |
| Last touch before consultation | 30% |
| Post-consultation touches (proposal follow-up) | 10% |

### UTM Strategy

Every link ALLONE publishes — in social posts, emails, ads, press releases, partner communications — carries UTM parameters.

**UTM naming convention:**

```
utm_source    = [platform]         → linkedin, google, newsletter, forbes-georgia
utm_medium    = [channel type]     → paid, organic, email, referral, pr
utm_campaign  = [campaign name]    → q1-2026-lead-gen, case-study-tbc, webinar-ai-basics
utm_content   = [creative variant] → carousel-v1, video-v2, cta-book-call
utm_term      = [keyword/audience] → ai-automation-georgia, cto-50-500
```

**Example URLs:**

- LinkedIn organic post: `allone.ge/blog/ai-automation-guide?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=blog-promo&utm_content=founder-post`
- Google Ads: `allone.ge/services/chatbot?utm_source=google&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=q1-chatbot-search&utm_term=chatbot-development-georgia`
- Newsletter: `allone.ge/case-study/tbc?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=issue-12&utm_content=cta-button`

### Reporting Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Key Metrics | Audience |
|--------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Channel performance | Weekly | Impressions, clicks, leads by source | Marketing |
| Pipeline attribution | Bi-weekly | MQLs, SQLs, pipeline value by first/last touch | Marketing + Sales |
| CAC by channel | Monthly | Cost per lead, cost per SQL, CAC by source | Founder + Marketing |
| PESO effectiveness | Quarterly | Revenue attributed to each PESO category, ROI by channel | Founder |

## 9. Integration Points — How PESO Channels Feed Each Other

The power of PESO is not in running four separate strategies but in creating feedback loops where each channel amplifies the others. Below is the integration map for ALLONE.

### Owned Feeds Everything

```
Blog Post (Owned)
  → Excerpted as LinkedIn post (Shared)
  → Excerpted as newsletter content (Owned)
  → Promoted via LinkedIn Ads to lookalike audience (Paid)
  → Pitched to journalist as evidence of expertise (Earned)
  → Retargeted to non-converting visitors (Paid)
```

### Earned Amplifies Credibility

```
Press Article (Earned)
  → "As featured in Forbes Georgia" on website (Owned)
  → Shared on LinkedIn with commentary (Shared)
  → Used as social proof in ad creative (Paid)
  → Included in email signature and newsletter (Owned)
  → Added to sales deck and proposal materials (Owned)
```

### Shared Generates Signals

```
LinkedIn Post Gets High Engagement (Shared)
  → Signals topic resonance → Write full blog post (Owned)
  → Comments reveal prospect pain points → Inform ad copy (Paid)
  → Engagement from journalists → Pitch follow-up (Earned)
  → Top-performing posts → Boost with ad spend (Paid)
```

### Paid Fills Gaps

```
Retargeting Ad (Paid)
  → Drives return visit to case study page (Owned)
  → Visitor subscribes to newsletter (Owned)
  → Newsletter nurtures to consultation booking (Owned)
  → Closed deal becomes new case study (Owned → Shared → Earned)
```

### The Virtuous Cycle

The ultimate integration is the closed loop: a client acquired through the PESO system becomes content for the system. Every successful project yields a case study (owned), a LinkedIn story (shared), a potential press angle (earned), and retargeting proof point (paid). As ALLONE's client portfolio grows, the PESO system becomes self-fueling — each client making the next one easier to acquire.

**Quarterly integration review checklist:**

- [ ] Is every blog post being distributed across at least 3 channels?
- [ ] Are high-performing social posts being promoted with paid spend?
- [ ] Is earned media coverage being repurposed across owned and shared channels?
- [ ] Are paid campaign insights informing content strategy for owned media?
- [ ] Is the sales team using PESO assets (case studies, press mentions, blog posts) in their outreach?
- [ ] Are closed deals being systematically converted into new PESO content?

## Summary

ALLONE's PESO strategy is built on one principle: create once, distribute everywhere, measure everything. The Georgian market advantage — accessible media, concentrated business community, low ad costs — provides an affordable testing ground for content and campaigns that can then scale into the EU market. By investing heavily in owned and shared media in Year 1, ALLONE builds assets that compound in value. By Year 2, earned credibility and proven paid campaigns accelerate growth without proportional budget increases. The attribution model ensures every dollar is tracked from first impression to closed deal, enabling continuous optimization of the entire system.

The competitive moat is not any single channel but the integration between all four. Competitors running isolated LinkedIn ads or sporadic PR campaigns cannot match the compounding effect of a fully integrated PESO operation where every piece of content works across multiple channels and every client success feeds back into the acquisition engine.
