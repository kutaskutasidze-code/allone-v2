## Goal-Setting Framework

ALLONE operates on a hybrid SMART + OKR framework. SMART goals define the concrete destinations — specific revenue targets, client counts, geographic milestones. OKRs define the qualitative objectives and the measurable key results that track momentum toward those destinations. Together they eliminate the two failure modes that kill young agencies: vagueness and tunnel vision.

Every goal in this document passes a five-point filter before it earns a place on the roadmap.

**Specific** — The goal names an exact outcome, not a direction. "Grow revenue" is not a goal. "$50K MRR by September 2026" is a goal.

**Measurable** — Every goal has a number attached. If it cannot be measured, it cannot be managed. Vanity metrics are excluded. The numbers here connect directly to business survival and market position.

**Achievable** — Ambitious does not mean delusional. Each target is stress-tested against current resources, market conditions, and historical growth rates. The 3x client growth in ninety days is aggressive but grounded in the existing pipeline and the thirty commission-based salespeople already in the field.

**Relevant** — Every goal traces back to one of three strategic imperatives: close the funding round, establish market leadership in Georgia, and build repeatable revenue. Goals that do not serve these imperatives are distractions.

**Time-bound** — Every goal has a deadline. Deadlines create urgency. Urgency creates focus. Focus creates results.

The OKR layer adds a second dimension. Where SMART goals answer "what exactly are we achieving," OKRs answer "what does excellence look like across the business." Quarterly OKRs ensure that the pursuit of one goal does not cannibalize another. Revenue growth without brand building is fragile. Brand building without operational discipline is theater.

## Quarterly OKR Framework

Each quarter ALLONE sets four objectives with three key results each. Objectives are qualitative and ambitious. Key results are quantitative and binary — they are either achieved or not.

### Q2 2026 OKRs: April through June

**Objective 1: Establish Market Leadership**
- KR1: Close 10 new clients (5 to 15 total)
- KR2: Publish 12 case studies demonstrating measurable client outcomes
- KR3: Sign first EU client through Belgium operations

**Objective 2: Accelerate Revenue Growth**
- KR1: Reach $20K MRR by June 30
- KR2: Close the $1.24M funding round by May 31
- KR3: Achieve average contract value above $2,000/month for new clients

**Objective 3: Build Brand Authority**
- KR1: Launch LinkedIn presence and reach 500 combined followers
- KR2: Publish 36 LinkedIn posts (3 per week for 12 weeks)
- KR3: Generate 5 inbound inquiries attributed to content marketing

**Objective 4: Achieve Operational Excellence**
- KR1: Average project delivery time under 3 weeks for standard engagements
- KR2: Client satisfaction score above 8.5 out of 10
- KR3: Formalize delivery playbook with documented processes for each service type

### OKR Template for Future Quarters

Each subsequent quarter follows the same structure. At the start of each quarter the leadership team scores the previous quarter's OKRs on a 0.0 to 1.0 scale (where 0.7 is considered a strong result) and sets new OKRs that build on the momentum.

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Objective** | Qualitative, ambitious, inspirational. One sentence. |
| **Key Result 1** | Quantitative, binary, stretch target. |
| **Key Result 2** | Quantitative, binary, stretch target. |
| **Key Result 3** | Quantitative, binary, stretch target. |
| **Score** | 0.0–1.0 assessed at quarter end. 0.7+ is strong. 1.0 means the target was too easy. |

## Review Cadence

Goals without reviews become wallpaper. The following cadence ensures that every metric is examined, every deviation is explained, and every course correction happens before momentum is lost.

### Weekly Review — Every Monday

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Participants:** Founder + delivery lead + sales lead
**Agenda:**
- Pipeline update: new leads, proposals sent, deals closed
- Delivery status: active projects, blockers, client escalations
- Revenue snapshot: current MRR, invoices outstanding, cash position
- Content performance: posts published, engagement metrics, follower growth
- Action items: 3 to 5 priorities for the week ahead

### Monthly Review — First Friday of Each Month

**Duration:** 90 minutes
**Participants:** Full team
**Agenda:**
- Month-over-month KPI comparison across all five metric categories
- Client health review: retention, satisfaction, upsell opportunities
- Pipeline deep-dive: conversion rates, average deal size, sales cycle length
- Budget review: actual spend versus plan, burn rate, runway remaining
- Marketing effectiveness: content ROI, lead source analysis, brand sentiment
- Goal progress: percentage completion against 90-day and 6-month targets
- Strategic adjustments: any pivots, new opportunities, or threats identified

### Quarterly Review — Last Week of Each Quarter

**Duration:** Half-day session
**Participants:** Full team plus advisors or board members if applicable
**Agenda:**
- OKR scoring: each key result scored 0.0 to 1.0 with candid assessment
- SMART goal evaluation: each goal marked as achieved, partially achieved, or missed with root cause analysis
- Financial review: quarterly P&L, cash flow, unit economics trends
- Competitive landscape: market changes, new entrants, partner movements
- Strategic planning: set next quarter's OKRs and adjust 6-month and 12-month targets based on actuals
- Team health: hiring progress, retention, capacity planning, skill gaps
- Investor update preparation: draft quarterly investor communication
