# 25. Marketing Strategy

## ALLONE Marketing Strategy 2026-2027

---

## 1. Marketing Overview

ALLONE operates at the intersection of two powerful market forces: the global acceleration of AI adoption and the emergence of Georgia as a credible technology hub. Our marketing strategy is built to exploit this convergence, positioning ALLONE not as another agency selling services, but as the definitive partner for businesses ready to operationalize AI.

### Strategic Marketing Approach

The marketing function at ALLONE serves three interconnected purposes. First, it must establish brand authority in a market flooded with AI noise — every consultancy, freelancer, and SaaS company now claims AI expertise, which means differentiation requires substance, not slogans. Second, it must generate qualified leads at a cost structure that reflects our AI-native delivery advantage — if we reduce delivery costs by 90%, our marketing should reflect that same efficiency and precision. Third, it must build a compounding asset: a body of content, relationships, and brand equity that appreciates over time rather than depreciating with each campaign cycle.

Our approach is founder-led, content-first, and community-driven. In B2B services, trust precedes transactions. Buyers do not fill out contact forms because they saw a banner ad. They reach out because they consumed a piece of content that reframed their understanding, because a founder's post resonated with a challenge they face, or because a peer recommended the company. Every marketing dollar and hour we invest must map to one of these trust-building pathways.

### Market Context

The global AI services market is projected to exceed $200 billion by 2028. Within the Georgian market, digital transformation spending is accelerating as EU association agreements drive regulatory modernization and competitive pressure. In Belgium and the broader EU, SMBs face a dual challenge: they understand that AI is essential for competitiveness, yet most lack the internal capability to implement it. ALLONE's positioning — affordable, proven, AI-native — addresses this gap directly.

### Core Marketing Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Substance over spectacle** | Every piece of content must teach, demonstrate, or prove. No empty thought leadership. |
| **Founder as the front door** | The founder's personal brand is the single highest-leverage marketing asset. Invest accordingly. |
| **Proof density** | Case studies, ROI numbers, live demos, and client testimonials carry more weight than any copy. |
| **Compounding distribution** | Build owned channels (email list, blog, LinkedIn following) that grow in value month over month. |
| **Dual-market fluency** | All marketing must work across Georgian and European audiences without feeling generic to either. |
| **Efficiency as brand** | Our own use of AI in marketing production is itself a demonstration of what we sell. |

## 3. Channel Strategy

### 3.1 LinkedIn — Primary Channel (75-85% of B2B Leads)

LinkedIn is the single most important marketing channel for ALLONE. In B2B services — particularly AI and technology consulting — LinkedIn is where decision-makers research, evaluate, and shortlist potential partners. Our strategy treats LinkedIn not as a social media platform but as a business development engine.

#### Company Page Strategy

The ALLONE company page serves as the institutional anchor. It validates the founder's personal content with organizational credibility and provides a hub for job seekers, partners, and enterprise buyers who need to see a "real company" behind the founder's posts.

**Posting cadence:** 4-5 posts per week
**Content mix:**
- 30% case studies and client results (anonymized where necessary)
- 25% AI education and industry insights
- 20% company culture and team highlights
- 15% service announcements and product updates
- 10% curated industry news with ALLONE perspective

**Page optimization checklist:**
- Custom banner reflecting current campaign or positioning
- Complete "About" section with keyword-rich description
- Featured section with top 3 case studies or assets
- All employees linked and listed
- Custom CTA button directing to booking page

#### Founder Personal Brand (Detailed in Section 6)

The founder's personal LinkedIn presence drives 60-70% of all LinkedIn-sourced leads. This is the highest-ROI marketing activity in the entire strategy.

#### Employee Advocacy Program

With 30 commission-based salespeople, ALLONE has an unusual distribution advantage. Each salesperson becomes a content distribution node.

**Program structure:**
- Weekly content kit distributed every Monday: 2-3 pre-written posts, 1 article to share with suggested commentary, key talking points for the week
- Engagement pods: Employees commit to engaging (meaningful comments, not just likes) with founder and company posts within the first hour of publication
- Incentives: Monthly recognition for top-performing employee advocates, bonus structure tied to leads generated through personal LinkedIn activity
- Training: Monthly 30-minute session on LinkedIn best practices, profile optimization, and content creation

**Expected impact:** Each active employee advocate extends reach by 500-2,000 impressions per post. With 15-20 active advocates, this creates an amplification layer worth 10,000-40,000 additional impressions weekly at zero media cost.

#### Google Ads

**Budget:** 30-35% of total paid budget
**Campaign types:**
- **Search — branded:** Capture anyone searching "ALLONE" or "allone.ge" (defensive)
- **Search — high intent:** Target "AI automation agency," "chatbot development company," "AI consulting Tbilisi," "AI agency Belgium"
- **Search — competitor:** Bid on competitor brand names where legally permissible

**Keyword strategy:**
- Focus exclusively on bottom-funnel, high commercial intent keywords
- Negative keyword list maintained weekly to eliminate waste
- Landing pages matched 1:1 to ad groups for maximum Quality Score

- No campaign runs for more than 14 days without performance review
- Minimum ROAS target: 5x within 90 days of lead generation
- Kill any campaign with CPL (cost per lead) exceeding 3x the target within 30 days
- Monthly budget cap with automatic pause at threshold

## 4. Content Strategy

### Content Pillars

Content pillars ensure consistency and prevent the common B2B trap of publishing random, disconnected pieces. Every content asset maps to one of five pillars.

#### Pillar 1: AI Education (30% of content)

Demystify AI for business audiences. Remove fear, build understanding, create demand.

**Topics:**
- What AI automation actually means for a 20-person company
- The real cost of not automating in 2026
- AI glossary for business leaders (not technical jargon)
- "Will AI replace my team?" — honest answers
- How to evaluate AI solutions (buyer's guide)

**Formats:** Blog posts, LinkedIn carousels, short explainer videos, webinar presentations

#### Pillar 2: Case Studies and Proof (25% of content)

Concrete demonstrations of ALLONE's impact. Numbers, timelines, and client context.

**Topics:**
- Full case studies (problem, solution, results, ROI)
- Before/after comparisons with specific metrics
- Client video testimonials
- Industry-specific implementation guides
- "How we built X in Y days" technical breakdowns

**Formats:** Long-form blog posts, LinkedIn posts with metrics, video testimonials, PDF downloads

#### Pillar 3: Industry Insights (20% of content)

Position ALLONE as a company that understands where AI is going, not just where it is today.

**Topics:**
- Weekly AI news commentary (what it means for SMBs)
- Industry trend reports (quarterly)
- Georgian tech ecosystem analysis
- EU AI regulation implications for businesses
- Competitive landscape updates

**Formats:** Newsletter features, LinkedIn posts, blog analysis pieces, podcast guest appearances

#### Pillar 4: Behind the Scenes (15% of content)

Humanize the company. Show the team, the process, the culture.

**Topics:**
- Team member spotlights
- Office life in Tbilisi and Belgium
- How ALLONE uses AI internally
- Project process walk-throughs
- Company milestones and celebrations

**Formats:** LinkedIn photo/video posts, Instagram stories (if activated), blog posts, short video clips

#### Pillar 5: Founder Journey (10% of content)

The founder's personal narrative as a vehicle for brand values and audience connection.

**Topics:**
- Lessons from building ALLONE at 22
- Fundraising journey (transparent, with numbers where appropriate)
- Georgia's startup ecosystem from the inside
- Decision-making frameworks and mental models
- Failures, pivots, and real talk

**Formats:** LinkedIn personal posts, long-form blog essays, podcast interviews, speaking engagements

### Content Types and Production Standards

| Content Type | Frequency | Length/Duration | Production Time | Distribution |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn posts (founder)** | 3-5/week | 150-300 words | 30-60 min each | LinkedIn |
| **LinkedIn posts (company)** | 4-5/week | 100-250 words | 30-45 min each | LinkedIn |
| **Blog posts** | 2-4/month | 1,500-3,000 words | 4-8 hours each | Website, LinkedIn, newsletter |
| **Case studies** | 1-2/month | 1,000-2,000 words | 6-10 hours each | Website, LinkedIn, sales collateral |
| **Newsletter** | Bi-weekly | 800-1,200 words | 3-4 hours each | Email |
| **Video content** | 2-4/month | 1-5 minutes | 2-6 hours each | LinkedIn, YouTube, website |
| **Webinars** | Monthly | 30-60 minutes | 10-15 hours each | Zoom/platform, recorded for YouTube |
| **Templates/tools** | Quarterly | Varies | 10-20 hours each | Website (gated), LinkedIn promotion |

### Content Calendar Framework — Weekly Rhythm

**Monday — Strategy Day**
- Review previous week's performance metrics
- Finalize this week's content lineup
- Distribute employee advocacy kit
- Schedule all pre-produced content

**Tuesday — Publish Day 1**
- Founder LinkedIn post (AI insight or industry commentary)
- Company LinkedIn post (case study snippet or service highlight)
- Newsletter send (bi-weekly Tuesdays)

**Wednesday — Production Day**
- Write and edit blog content
- Record video content
- Design visual assets
- Prepare webinar materials

**Thursday — Publish Day 2**
- Founder LinkedIn post (personal story or behind-the-scenes)
- Company LinkedIn post (educational content or team highlight)
- Blog post published (if scheduled this week)

**Friday — Publish Day 3 and Community**
- Founder LinkedIn post (weekly wrap-up, question to audience, or curated insight)
- Engage heavily in comments and DMs
- Respond to all pending inquiries from the week
- Identify collaboration and guest content opportunities

### Content Production Process

**Step 1 — Ideation (Ongoing)**
Maintain a rolling content backlog in the project management tool. Sources: client conversations, industry news, competitor analysis, audience questions, internal team insights, sales objection patterns.

**Step 2 — Briefing (Weekly)**
Each content piece gets a brief: target audience, pillar, primary keyword (if SEO-relevant), key message, CTA, format, and distribution plan.

**Step 3 — Creation (Per Schedule)**
AI-assisted first drafts for efficiency. Human editing for voice, accuracy, and nuance. All content reviewed for brand consistency before publication.

**Step 4 — Review (Before Publication)**
Two-person review minimum for long-form content. Founder reviews all content tied to personal brand. Legal review for case studies involving client data.

**Step 5 — Distribution (Per Calendar)**
Primary channel publication, cross-promotion on secondary channels, employee advocacy activation, paid amplification for top performers.

**Step 6 — Analysis (Weekly)**
Track performance against benchmarks. Identify patterns. Double down on what works. Kill what does not.

## 6. Founder Personal Brand Strategy

The founder and company brands must reinforce each other without becoming redundant.

**Rules:**
- Founder posts are personal, opinionated, and story-driven
- Company posts are institutional, data-driven, and service-oriented
- Founder references company naturally ("We just shipped X for a client") but does not pitch
- Company reposts founder content with institutional commentary, not just shares
- Joint campaigns: Founder introduces topic, company follows with deep-dive
- Event appearances always tagged with company page

4. **Reinvest wins.** Revenue generated from marketing-sourced leads funds next month's marketing expansion.

### Attribution Model

ALLONE uses a multi-touch attribution model to accurately credit marketing activities with revenue outcomes.

**First-touch attribution:** Identifies which channel first brought the lead into the ecosystem. Critical for understanding top-of-funnel effectiveness.

**Last-touch attribution:** Identifies which channel directly preceded conversion. Critical for understanding bottom-of-funnel effectiveness.

**Linear attribution:** Distributes credit equally across all touchpoints. Used for overall channel valuation.

**Implementation:**
- UTM parameters on all external links (mandatory, no exceptions)
- CRM tracks original source, most recent source, and all intermediate touchpoints
- Monthly attribution review to identify channel synergies and optimize spend
- Quarterly model calibration to ensure accuracy as channels mature

### Performance Review Protocol

1. **Green zone (on target or above):** Continue current approach, test incremental improvements
2. **Yellow zone (within 20% of target):** Diagnose root cause, implement specific fixes, review in 2 weeks
3. **Red zone (more than 20% below target):** Immediate strategy review, reallocate budget, consider channel pivot or team adjustment
