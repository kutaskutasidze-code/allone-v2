## 1. Social Media Overview

### Why Social Media Matters for a B2B AI Agency

The B2B buying cycle has fundamentally shifted. Decision-makers no longer wait for a sales pitch — they research vendors, evaluate credibility, and form opinions long before they ever fill out a contact form. For an AI automation agency like ALLONE, social media is not a vanity channel. It is the front line of trust-building, education, and pipeline generation.

The AI industry suffers from a credibility gap. Every agency claims to "leverage AI" — most cannot demonstrate real results. Social media gives ALLONE the stage to prove competence through consistent, substantive content that separates genuine expertise from hollow marketing. When a CFO in Brussels sees a detailed breakdown of how ALLONE cut a client's invoice processing time by 80%, that post does more selling than any cold email ever could.

**Three strategic functions of social media for ALLONE:**

1. **Demand Generation** — Creating awareness and interest among businesses that have not yet recognized their need for AI automation. Educational content plants seeds that mature into inbound leads weeks or months later.

2. **Trust & Authority Building** — Establishing ALLONE as the credible, knowledgeable partner in AI automation. Case studies, technical insights, and transparent founder storytelling signal competence in a market saturated with noise.

3. **Relationship Infrastructure** — Social platforms are where partnerships, investor connections, talent recruitment, and ecosystem positioning happen organically. For a 22-year-old founder building an international agency from Tbilisi, social presence is the great equalizer.

### Platform Selection Rationale

ALLONE's target clients are business owners, operations managers, CTOs, and decision-makers at SMBs and mid-market companies. The platform selection reflects where these people spend professional attention and where B2B purchase decisions are influenced.

| Platform | Role | Effort Allocation | Primary Audience |
|----------|------|-------------------|------------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Primary lead generation & thought leadership | 80% | B2B decision-makers, investors, partners |
| **Instagram** | Brand personality & culture | 10% | Talent, creative community, brand awareness |
| **Facebook** | Local Georgian market presence | 5% | Georgian business owners, local ecosystem |
| **Twitter/X** | Tech community engagement | 5% | Developers, AI practitioners, tech founders |

LinkedIn dominates allocation because the data is unambiguous: 75-85% of ALLONE's B2B leads originate from or are influenced by LinkedIn activity. This is not a guess — it is the measured reality of where the target audience lives professionally. Every other platform serves a supporting role: Instagram humanizes the brand, Facebook captures the local Georgian market that still defaults to Facebook for business discovery, and Twitter/X keeps ALLONE visible in the global tech conversation.

## 2. Platform Strategy

### 2.1 LinkedIn — Primary Channel (80% of Effort)

LinkedIn is ALLONE's revenue engine on social. Every piece of content published here should serve one of four objectives: generate leads, build authority, nurture existing prospects, or attract talent.

#### Company Page Strategy

The ALLONE LinkedIn company page operates as a content hub organized around four recurring themes:

**Thought Leadership (35% of company posts)**
Original perspectives on AI automation trends, industry shifts, and the future of work. These posts position ALLONE as a voice worth following, not just a service provider. Example: a post breaking down why 70% of enterprise AI projects fail and what SMBs can learn from those mistakes.

**Case Studies & Results (30% of company posts)**
Concrete, numbers-driven stories of client transformations. Every case study follows a consistent structure: the problem, the approach, the measurable result, and the broader lesson. Anonymize client details when necessary, but always lead with real metrics. "We reduced manual data entry by 12 hours per week for a logistics company" outperforms "We help companies automate" every single time.

**Service Highlights (20% of company posts)**
Educational breakdowns of what ALLONE actually builds — chatbots, custom AI solutions, workflow automation, websites, consulting. These posts translate technical capability into business outcomes. Avoid jargon. Speak to the pain point, not the technology.

**Company Culture (15% of company posts)**
Team introductions, office moments, company milestones, hiring announcements. These posts attract talent and humanize the agency. Show the Tbilisi office, the Belgium expansion, the energy of a young team building something ambitious.

#### Posting Cadence

| Day | Post Type | Format | Objective |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Monday | Industry insight / AI trend | Text post | Thought leadership |
| Tuesday | Case study / client result | Carousel or text | Lead generation |
| Wednesday | Educational how-to | Carousel or article | Authority building |
| Thursday | Company culture / team | Image + text or video | Brand humanization |
| Friday | Engagement / question / poll | Text post or poll | Community building |

**Target: 4-5 posts per week.** Consistency matters more than perfection. A steady cadence of quality content outperforms sporadic viral attempts.

#### Content Type Mix

| Format | Allocation | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Text posts** | 60% | Highest organic reach on LinkedIn, fastest to produce, best for thought leadership |
| **Carousels** | 20% | Strong engagement for educational content, case study breakdowns, process explainers |
| **Video** | 10% | Founder commentary, client testimonials, behind-the-scenes — high engagement but higher production cost |
| **Articles** | 10% | Long-form deep dives, SEO value, establishes depth of expertise |

#### Best-Performing Content Formats

Based on B2B LinkedIn benchmarks and early ALLONE data, these formats consistently outperform:

**"AI Tip of the Day" Series**
Short, actionable tips that business owners can implement immediately. Example: "AI Tip: You can automate 90% of your invoice matching with a simple workflow. Here's the 3-step setup..." These posts have high save rates and attract followers who later become leads.

**Before/After Automation Stories**
Visual or narrative comparisons showing a process before and after ALLONE's intervention. "Before: 3 people spent 15 hours/week on data entry. After: Automated pipeline processes 2,000 entries in 4 minutes." Concrete transformation stories are the highest-converting content type for service businesses.

**Founder Insights**
Personal posts from the founder's perspective — lessons learned, honest reflections on building an AI agency at 22, observations from the intersection of technology and business. These posts humanize the brand and generate the most comments and shares.

**Contrarian Takes**
Posts that challenge conventional wisdom in the AI space. "Most businesses don't need a custom AI model. They need a $50/month automation workflow." Contrarian framing stops the scroll and generates debate — which the algorithm rewards.

#### Engagement Strategy

Posting is half the equation. Active engagement is what transforms followers into a community and a community into a pipeline.

- **Comment on 10-15 relevant industry posts daily.** Prioritize posts from potential clients, partners, investors, and industry voices. Leave substantive comments — not "Great post!" but genuine insights that add value.
- **Participate in AI/tech LinkedIn groups.** Answer questions, share resources, and establish presence in groups where target clients gather.
- **Respond to every comment on ALLONE posts within 2 hours.** Every comment is a conversation opportunity. The algorithm also rewards posts with active comment threads.
- **DM follow-up on warm engagements.** When someone comments on multiple posts or engages consistently, initiate a genuine conversation via DM — not a sales pitch, but a relationship-building touch.

#### LinkedIn Ads

Organic reach is the foundation. Paid amplification accelerates results for the highest-performing content.

**Sponsored Content for Lead Generation**
- Promote top-performing organic posts to targeted audiences (operations managers, CTOs, business owners in target industries and geographies).
- Lead gen forms within LinkedIn for gated content (AI readiness assessments, automation ROI calculators, case study PDFs).
- Budget: Start with $500-1,000/month, scale based on cost-per-lead results.

**Retargeting Website Visitors**
- Install the LinkedIn Insight Tag on allone.ge.
- Create retargeting audiences of website visitors who did not convert.
- Serve these audiences case studies and social proof content to nurture them back into the funnel.
- Retargeting typically delivers 3-5x better conversion rates than cold targeting.

**Audience Targeting Parameters**
- Job titles: CEO, COO, CTO, VP Operations, Director of IT, Business Owner
- Company size: 10-500 employees (SMB and mid-market)
- Industries: Logistics, finance, retail, professional services, manufacturing
- Geography: Belgium, Netherlands, Germany, Georgia, UK (phased expansion)

### 2.2 Instagram — Secondary Channel (10% of Effort)

Instagram serves a fundamentally different purpose than LinkedIn. It is not a lead generation tool — it is a brand personality vehicle. Instagram answers the question: "What kind of company is ALLONE?"

#### Purpose

Show the human side of a technology company. Feature the team, the office environment, the creative process, and the design sensibility that sets ALLONE apart. Instagram also serves talent recruitment — talented designers and developers want to work at companies that look and feel exciting.

#### Content Strategy

**Feed Posts (3x per week)**
- Office life and team moments (the Tbilisi office, the Belgium setup)
- Design showcases (website builds, UI/UX work, before/after visuals)
- Process shots (whiteboard sessions, code on screens, client workshops)
- Milestone announcements (new clients, team hires, office expansions)

**Stories (daily)**
- Quick behind-the-scenes clips
- Polls and questions to drive engagement
- Reposts of team member content
- Event coverage (conferences, meetups, tech events in Tbilisi)

**Reels (1-2x per week)**
- 30-60 second explainers of AI concepts
- Time-lapse of a project going from concept to launch
- Day-in-the-life content from team members
- Trending audio with ALLONE-relevant hooks

#### Aesthetic Guidelines

Every Instagram post must align with ALLONE's Cold Clarity design language:
- **Background:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) or tinted surface (#F8FAFE)
- **Accent color:** Electric Blue (#0A68F5) as the signature pop
- **Typography:** General Sans for headlines, clean and geometric
- **Photography:** High contrast, natural lighting, minimal clutter
- **No:** Busy gradients, purple AI cliches, stock photography, generic tech imagery

Create a set of 10-15 Instagram templates in Figma that the team can populate quickly. Consistency of visual identity is non-negotiable.

Reach Georgian business owners who discover services through Facebook pages and groups. Maintain a professional presence for the local market while the international strategy runs primarily through LinkedIn.

#### Content Strategy

#### Posting Cadence

2-3 posts per week. Repurpose LinkedIn content with Georgian-language adaptation — do not create entirely separate content.

## 3. Founder Personal Brand Plan

The founder's personal brand is ALLONE's most powerful marketing asset. A 22-year-old building an international AI agency from Tbilisi is inherently a compelling story. The personal brand strategy turns that story into a systematic pipeline generator.

### LinkedIn Profile Optimization

**Headline:** Not a job title — a value proposition.
Example: "Building ALLONE — AI automation that cuts business costs by 90% | Tbilisi to Brussels"

**About Section:** A concise narrative covering:
- What ALLONE does and who it serves
- The founder's unique perspective (youth, Georgia, intersection of philosophy and technology)
- A clear call to action (DM for conversation, visit allone.ge)

**Featured Section:** Pin the three highest-performing posts, a link to allone.ge, and one piece of long-form content (article or case study PDF).

**Banner Image:** Custom designed in Cold Clarity style — ALLONE branding, clean typography, subtle Electric Blue accent.

**Activity:** The profile must show consistent posting activity. An optimized but inactive profile is a wasted asset.

### Personal Posting Strategy

**Frequency:** 3-5 personal posts per week (in addition to company page content).

**Voice:** Direct, substantive, occasionally philosophical. Avoid corporate-speak. Write like a person who builds things and thinks deeply, not like a marketing department.

**Content Themes:**

| Theme | Frequency | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| AI Future | 30% | Original perspectives on where AI is heading and what it means for business |
| Founder Journey | 25% | Honest stories about building from Georgia, early mistakes, wins, lessons |
| Business Insights | 20% | Observations about markets, pricing, hiring, strategy — applicable beyond AI |
| Philosophy/Art Crossover | 15% | Connecting deeper thinking to business decisions — the INNRBURIAL intellectual DNA |
| Industry Commentary | 10% | Hot takes on AI news, product launches, market shifts |

### Speaking & Webinar Appearances

Build a speaking calendar to amplify reach beyond social feeds:

- **Target 1 webinar/podcast appearance per month** in Year 1, scaling to 2-3 per month in Year 2.
- **Topics:** AI automation for SMBs, building a tech company from Georgia, the future of work with AI, founder journey content.
- **Target platforms:** Industry podcasts, LinkedIn Live sessions, tech conference panels, Startup Georgia events, European tech meetups.
- **Preparation:** Develop 3 signature talks that can be adapted for different audiences: (1) "The 90% Cost Drop: How AI Changes the Economics of Business Operations," (2) "Building Global from Tbilisi: Why Geography No Longer Determines Destiny," (3) "AI Without the Hype: What Actually Works for Mid-Market Companies."

### Relationship Building

Social media is not broadcasting — it is networking at scale.

- **Identify 50 key accounts** (potential clients, partners, investors, industry voices) and engage with their content consistently for 90 days before any outreach.
- **Build genuine connections with other founders.** Comment on their posts, share their content, celebrate their wins. This creates reciprocal visibility and referral networks.
- **Investor visibility.** Engage with VCs and angels who invest in AI/SaaS. Share fundraising milestones (with appropriate discretion) and company growth updates that demonstrate traction.

### Cross-Promotion with Company Page

The founder's personal posts and the company page should amplify each other:
- Founder shares and comments on company posts, driving personal network traffic to the company page.
- Company page reposts or references founder content, adding institutional weight to personal insights.
- Coordinate timing — do not post competing content from both accounts simultaneously. Stagger by at least 4 hours.

## 5. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: "AI Demystified" — 40% of Content

**Purpose:** Make AI automation accessible and understandable for non-technical business owners. Strip away the hype and show what AI actually does in practical terms.

**Content Examples:**
- "5 Tasks You're Doing Manually That AI Can Handle in 5 Minutes"
- "AI vs. Automation: What's the Difference and Why It Matters for Your Business"
- "The Real Cost of NOT Automating: A Calculator for SMBs"
- "AI Tip of the Day" recurring series
- Myth-busting posts ("No, AI Won't Replace Your Entire Team — Here's What It Will Do")

**Tone:** Approachable, jargon-free, practical. Speak to business outcomes, not technical architecture.

**Formats:** Text posts (60%), carousels (30%), short articles (10%).

### Pillar 2: "Transformation Stories" — 25% of Content

**Purpose:** Show, don't tell. Real client results with real numbers are the most compelling sales tool in B2B marketing.

**Content Examples:**
- "How a Logistics Company Saved 60 Hours Per Month with One Automation"
- Before/after process flow comparisons
- Client video testimonials (even 60-second informal clips)
- ROI breakdowns with specific numbers
- "What We Learned from This Project" — honest reflections on challenging implementations

**Tone:** Results-focused, specific, honest. Include challenges and lessons alongside wins.

**Formats:** Carousels (40%), text posts with data (35%), video testimonials (15%), long-form articles (10%).

**Production Process:**
1. At project completion, conduct a 30-minute client debrief
2. Extract key metrics (time saved, cost reduced, revenue generated)
3. Draft the story with client approval for public sharing
4. Create visual assets (process diagrams, metric graphics)
5. Publish on LinkedIn, repurpose for website and other platforms

### Pillar 3: "Georgia Tech" — 15% of Content

**Purpose:** Position Tbilisi as a legitimate tech hub and ALLONE as its flagship AI company. This narrative serves two audiences: international clients who need reassurance about working with a Georgian company, and the local ecosystem that ALLONE wants to lead.

**Content Examples:**
- "Why We Built an AI Agency in Tbilisi (And Why You Should Pay Attention)"
- Spotlight posts on the Georgian tech ecosystem, startup events, and talent pool
- Collaboration stories with Georgian tech companies and partners
- Comparisons: cost of tech talent in Georgia vs. Western Europe (without being reductive)
- "From Tbilisi to Brussels" — the expansion narrative

**Tone:** Proud but not defensive. Frame Georgia as a strategic advantage, not a limitation to overcome.

**Formats:** Text posts (50%), images/video of Tbilisi tech scene (30%), articles (20%).

### Pillar 4: "Inside ALLONE" — 10% of Content

**Purpose:** Humanize the agency. Show the team, the culture, the process. People buy from people — especially in B2B services where trust is the deciding factor.

**Content Examples:**
- Team member introductions and "day in the life" features
- Office environment shots (Tbilisi and Belgium)
- Hiring announcements and "why I joined ALLONE" stories
- Company milestones (client count, revenue targets, expansion news)
- Process transparency: how a project goes from brief to delivery

**Tone:** Genuine, energetic, aspirational without being forced. Let the natural dynamism of a young, ambitious team speak for itself.

**Formats:** Images (40%), video/Reels (30%), text posts (30%).

### Pillar 5: "Founder's Lens" — 10% of Content

**Purpose:** The founder's unique perspective — building at 22, the intersection of philosophy and technology, navigating investor conversations, and thinking about business at a deeper level than the typical LinkedIn post.

**Content Examples:**
- "What Building a Company at 22 Taught Me About [Topic]"
- Connections between philosophical frameworks and business strategy
- Honest reflections on failures, pivots, and hard decisions
- Observations from the intersection of art, technology, and commerce
- Reading recommendations and intellectual influences

**Tone:** Reflective, intellectually honest, personal. This is not company content — it is individual thought leadership that happens to benefit the company.

**Formats:** Text posts (80%), occasional long-form articles (20%).

## 6. Community Management

### Response Time Standards

| Platform | Target Response Time | Maximum Response Time |
|----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **LinkedIn** | 1 hour | 2 hours |
| **Instagram** | 2 hours | 4 hours |
| **Facebook** | 2 hours | 4 hours |
| **Twitter/X** | 1 hour | 3 hours |

Response times apply during business hours (9:00-19:00 GET). Outside business hours, respond within the first hour of the next business day. Exceptions: urgent client issues or PR-sensitive mentions should be flagged immediately via Slack/internal channel.

### Engagement Playbook

**Positive Comments:**
- Thank the commenter by name.
- Add value by expanding on their point or asking a follow-up question.
- Never respond with generic "Thanks!" — each reply should be substantive.

**Questions:**
- Answer directly and thoroughly.
- If the question suggests a potential lead, answer publicly and then follow up via DM with an offer to discuss further.
- For complex technical questions, promise a follow-up post addressing the topic in detail.

**Criticism or Negative Feedback:**
- Acknowledge the concern professionally.
- Never delete negative comments unless they contain hate speech, spam, or personal attacks.
- Move detailed disputes to DM: "I appreciate you raising this — let me DM you so we can discuss in detail."
- If the criticism has merit, own it publicly: "You're right, and here's what we're doing about it."

**Competitor Mentions:**
- Never disparage competitors publicly.
- If someone compares ALLONE to a competitor, differentiate on value, not by criticizing others.
- Focus on ALLONE's unique positioning: AI-native, cost-efficient, results-driven.

**Spam and Trolls:**
- Hide or delete spam comments.
- Do not engage with trolls. If a troll thread gains visibility, respond once with a factual correction and disengage.

### Crisis Management Protocol

**Level 1 — Minor Issue** (negative review, unhappy comment):
- Respond within 1 hour with empathy and a solution.
- Escalate to account manager if client-related.
- Document in the incident log.

**Level 2 — Moderate Issue** (viral negative post, factual error gaining traction):
- Pause all scheduled content immediately.
- Draft a response with founder approval before publishing.
- Respond within 2 hours with a factual, empathetic statement.
- Monitor the conversation actively for 48 hours.

**Level 3 — Major Crisis** (data breach, legal issue, major public backlash):
- All social media posting stops immediately.
- Founder and legal counsel draft a formal statement.
- Publish statement on all channels within 4 hours.
- Designate a single spokesperson for all public communication.
- Daily monitoring and reporting until resolution.

**Crisis Response Template:**
"We're aware of [issue]. We take this seriously. Here's what we know so far: [facts]. Here's what we're doing: [actions]. We'll provide an update by [specific time]. If you're directly affected, please contact us at [email]."

### User-Generated Content Strategy

Encourage clients, partners, and team members to create content that mentions ALLONE:

- **Client testimonials:** After every successful project, ask clients if they would share a brief LinkedIn post about their experience. Provide a suggested structure but let them write in their own voice.
- **Team member content:** Encourage team members to post about their work, projects, and experience at ALLONE. Provide guidelines but do not script their posts.
- **Repost and amplify:** When someone mentions ALLONE positively, reshare with a thoughtful comment. This rewards the behavior and encourages others.
- **Partner co-creation:** Collaborate with partner companies on joint content — webinars, co-authored posts, shared case studies.

**UTM Convention:**
All links shared on social media must include UTM parameters:
- `utm_source`: platform name (linkedin, instagram, facebook, twitter)
- `utm_medium`: social
- `utm_campaign`: content-theme-month (e.g., ai-demystified-mar26)
- `utm_content`: post-type (e.g., carousel, text-post, video)

### Content Creation Process & Approval Flow

**Step 1: Ideation (Monday)**
- Review content calendar and quarterly theme
- Brainstorm post topics aligned with content pillars
- Check trending topics and industry news for timely hooks

**Step 2: Drafting (Monday-Tuesday)**
- Write post copy in a shared Google Doc or Notion workspace
- Create visual assets in Figma/Canva using brand templates
- Tag posts with content pillar, platform, and format

**Step 3: Review & Approval (Tuesday-Wednesday)**
- All company page content reviewed by brand lead before publishing
- Founder personal posts: self-approved (founder writes in own voice)
- Case studies and client-related content: client approval required before publishing

**Step 4: Scheduling (Wednesday)**
- Schedule the full week's content in Buffer
- Set reminders for manual engagement tasks (commenting, DMs)
- Queue Instagram Stories content

**Step 5: Publishing & Monitoring (Daily)**
- Monitor comments and messages per response time standards
- Track early engagement signals and boost high-performing posts organically (share to personal profile, engage in comments)

**Step 6: Analysis (Friday)**
- Log performance metrics in tracking spreadsheet
- Identify top performer and underperformer of the week
- Note learnings for next week's content planning
