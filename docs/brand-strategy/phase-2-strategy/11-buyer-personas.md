# 11. Buyer Personas

## Strategic Context

Understanding who buys from ALLONE is not a marketing exercise — it is the foundation of every decision we make, from service packaging to pricing to channel strategy. These five personas represent the primary archetypes we encounter across our two markets: Georgia and the European Union (Belgium as beachhead).

Each persona is built from real patterns observed in early client conversations, market research, and the competitive landscape. They are not hypothetical. They reflect the actual humans who will evaluate, purchase, and champion ALLONE's services.

These personas inform:
- **Sales scripts and objection handling** — what to say and when
- **Content strategy** — what to publish and where
- **Service packaging** — how to bundle offerings for maximum relevance
- **Pricing architecture** — what each segment can bear and what they expect
- **Channel allocation** — where to spend time and budget

---

## How to Use This Document

1. **Sales team**: Before any call, identify which persona the prospect most closely resembles. Adjust language, proof points, and pacing accordingly.
2. **Marketing team**: Map every piece of content to at least one persona. If it does not serve a persona, question whether it should exist.
3. **Product/delivery team**: When scoping a project, reference the persona's technical sophistication and decision style to calibrate communication and deliverable format.
4. **Leadership**: Use persona budget ranges and decision timelines to forecast pipeline velocity and revenue.

He gets home by 8 PM, exhausted. He scrolls Facebook and LinkedIn. He sees a post from another Georgian founder about using AI to automate customer service. He is intrigued but skeptical. He screenshots it and sends it to his business partner with the message: "Have you heard about this?"

### Decision Journey

**Awareness:** Giorgi becomes aware of AI automation through a Facebook post, a conversation at a networking event, or a friend's recommendation. He does not seek it out — it finds him through his network.

**Consideration:** He asks around. "Has anyone used this?" He looks at the company's website, specifically the pricing page and case studies. He wants to see a business like his — not a Fortune 500 example. He requests a demo call. During the call, he evaluates two things: does this person understand my business, and can they show me something working right now?

**Decision:** He decides within one to two weeks. He does not need a committee. He needs to feel confident that (a) the solution will actually work in his context, (b) his team can use it without a PhD, and (c) the price is fair relative to what he would spend on additional staff. A free pilot or a money-back guarantee accelerates his decision.
