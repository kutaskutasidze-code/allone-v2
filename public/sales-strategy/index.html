ASK THE RIGHT QUESTIONS

Discovery Questions

Click each product to reveal the questions that uncover real needs and close deals.

🌐

Website Discovery Questions

8 questions
▼
  1. What's your current website built on? When was it last updated?
  2. How many leads/inquiries does your website generate monthly?
  3. What's your current conversion rate? (Most businesses: 1-2%)
  4. Do you have someone managing content/updates in-house?
  5. What's your biggest frustration with your current site?
  6. How do customers currently find you online?
  7. Are you running any paid ads? Where do they land?
  8. What would a 3x improvement in web leads mean for revenue?
💬

AI Chatbot Discovery Questions

8 questions
▼
  1. How many customer inquiries does your business receive daily?
  2. What percentage of questions are repetitive / the same types?
  3. How many inquiries go unanswered or get delayed responses?
  4. What are the top 5 questions customers ask?
  5. Do you have staff handling inquiries outside business hours?
  6. How much does a lost customer cost you when they don't get a fast answer?
  7. How many support staff do you currently have and what's their cost?
  8. What channels do customers use to reach you? (Website, social, etc.)
🧠

AI Expert Discovery Questions

8 questions
▼
  1. How long does it take to onboard a new employee?
  2. Where does your company knowledge live? (Docs, emails, employee memory, scattered?)
  3. How often do people ask the same questions internally?
  4. Do you have SOPs/documentation? Is it up to date?
  5. What happens when a key employee leaves — does their knowledge leave too?
  6. How do new hires learn your processes today?
  7. How many hours per week are spent searching for information?
  8. Could your team make faster decisions if they had instant access to all company knowledge?

Objection Handling

Hover over any objection to reveal the response. Memorize these — they close deals.

✕

"It's too expensive"

"Let's look at it differently. You're spending $X on [current solution]. Our solution pays for itself in [timeframe]. The question isn't cost — it's ROI. Can I show you the math?"

✕

"We already have a website/system"

"That's great — most of our clients do. The question is: is it generating leads? Our clients typically see 3-5x more conversions after switching. What if we did a free audit of your current setup?"

✕

"We need to think about it"

"Absolutely, this is an important decision. What specific concerns would you like to think through? I can send a comparison doc that addresses [their concern]. When would be a good time to reconnect — Tuesday or Thursday?"

✕

"Can you do it cheaper?"

"I understand budget matters. Here's what I can do: we can start with the Basic package at $X, prove the ROI, then scale up. Most clients upgrade within 3 months because the results speak for themselves."

✕

"We tried AI before and it didn't work"

"That's actually common — 70% of first AI projects fail because of poor implementation, not bad technology. We've refined our process to avoid those pitfalls. Want to see how we approach it differently?"

✕

"I need to check with my boss/partner"

"Of course! Would it help if I prepared a one-page ROI summary they can review? Or better yet — can we schedule a 15-minute call with them? I'll keep it brief and focused on numbers."

✕

"We don't have time for this right now"

"I hear you — that's exactly why automation exists. The irony is that the businesses most 'too busy' are the ones that benefit most. What if I showed you how to save 20 hours/week without any disruption to your current workflow?"

✕

"How do I know this will work?"

"Fair question. Three things: 1) We offer a pilot phase so you see results before full commitment. 2) Here are 3 case studies from similar businesses. 3) If we don't hit agreed KPIs, we keep working until we do."

✕

"Your competitors are cheaper"

"They might be — and there's a reason. We use senior engineers and custom solutions, not templates. Ask them: do they build custom or use templates? What's their revision policy? Do they offer ongoing support? You get what you pay for."

✕

"We'll do it in-house"

"That's an option! Quick math though: a mid-level developer costs $40-60K/year plus benefits, takes 3-6 months to build what we deliver in 3-4 weeks. Plus ongoing maintenance. Our project cost is often less than 3 months of a developer's salary."

✕

"I've never heard of ALLONE"

"We're deliberately selective — we work with serious businesses, not everyone. Our team has delivered solutions for [reference clients]. We're young and hungry, which means you get senior-level attention at every step, not an account manager who forgets your name."

✕

"Send me a proposal and I'll review it"

"Happy to — but I want to make sure it's actually relevant. Can we do a quick 10-minute call so I understand your specific needs? That way the proposal addresses exactly what matters to you, not generic features."
